The Essential PCB Design Checklist: Pre-Flight Review

Designing a PCB is a meticulous process where a single misplaced via or an incorrect footprint can turn a $500 prototype run into expensive scrap metal. Before you export your Gerber files, running through a "Human Design Rule Check (DRC)" is the best way to catch mistakes that automated software tools often miss.

Schematic and Footprint Verification

The most common PCB failures aren't electrical—they're mechanical. Always verify that your component footprints match the physical parts you have ordered.

Print your PCB layout on a 1:1 scale on a standard piece of paper and physically place your components on the paper. This "paper prototyping" method catches incorrect pad spacing or overlapping components that look fine on a 27-inch monitor but are impossible to solder in real life. Pay special attention to polarized components like electrolytic capacitors and diodes.

Power Integrity and Grounding

Ensure your power traces are wide enough to handle the current without excessive voltage drop or heating.

Are your decoupling capacitors placed as close as possible to the IC power pins? If they are more than a few millimeters away, their effectiveness drops significantly due to trace inductance. Check your ground pours—ensure there are no "islands" of copper that aren't actually connected to the ground net. Use via stitching to tie ground planes on the top and bottom layers together to reduce noise and improve thermal dissipation.

Manufacturing Readiness (DFM)

Design for Manufacturing (DFM) ensures your board can be built reliably by a fabrication house.

  • Silkscreen: Is all text clear of solder masks and pads?
  • Drill Hits: Are your via holes large enough for the manufacturer's tolerances?
  • Edge Clearance: Keep traces at least 0.5mm away from the board edge to avoid damage during routing.
  • Mounting Holes: Do your mounting holes have enough clearance for screw heads without shorting nearby traces?

Engineering Pro Tip: Always use a Gerber Viewer (like Gerbv or an online tool) to inspect your exported files before sending them to the factory. Software glitches can sometimes cause missing copper or corrupt solder mask layers that don't show up in your EDA tool's layout editor.
